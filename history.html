History

Crochet has a rich history that traces its roots back to the 19th century. Though its exact origins are somewhat unclear, crochet is believed to have evolved from traditional needlework techniques. It gained popularity in Europe during the early 1800s, particularly in France and Ireland, where it was used to create intricate lacework and decorative items. The term "crochet" comes from the French word for "hook," reflecting the tool used in this craft. Throughout the 19th and early 20th centuries, crochet spread globally, becoming a beloved technique for creating both functional and decorative pieces. Its accessibility and versatility contributed to its widespread adoption. Today, crochet continues to evolve, with modern crafters experimenting with new patterns, yarns, and techniques, while still honoring its rich tradition and cultural significance.
